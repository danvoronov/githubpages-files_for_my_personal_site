**🎰 Social Media Feeds Are Like Slot Machines**
The video exposes how social media platforms use casino-like tactics to keep users hooked. By incorporating variable intermittent rewards, platforms like Instagram and TikTok exploit your brain's reward system, making it harder to stop scrolling.

**📢 Red Notifications = 🚨 Urgent Attention**
Ever noticed how notifications are often red? This is a deliberate psychological trick. Red triggers a sense of urgency, making us respond faster, even when the content isn't important. It's all about getting you back into the app as quickly as possible.

**🎲 The Dopamine Trap**
Much like pulling the lever on a slot machine, refreshing your feed or checking for notifications gives you a dopamine hit. But here's the twist: it's not about feeling good all the time. Social media apps thrive on making you anticipate a reward, just like a gambler waiting for a win.

**🧠 Psychology of Anticipation**
Studies show that dopamine spikes more when we anticipate a reward, not when we receive it. Social media companies know this and design their platforms to keep you in a state of constant anticipation, pulling you in again and again, like a gambler chasing a jackpot.

**🔁 Infinite Scrolling & Endless Feeds**
Just like slot machines, the endless feed on apps creates a loop you can’t easily escape from. The design is frictionless — no clear stopping point, no decision moment. It's meant to make you lose track of time and keep you mindlessly scrolling.
