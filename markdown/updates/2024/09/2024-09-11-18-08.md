**This video compares the lifestyles of people** earning $25,000, $40,000, $100,000, and $25 million per year in USA.

$25,000 (George): 🏢 Rents a small apartment outside the city, drives an old car, budgets carefully for food and healthcare. Struggles to make ends meet and relies on government assistance. 🚘 🍔 🏥

$40,000 (Simon): 🏡 Buys a small condo, has a decent car, can afford some entertainment and eating out. Still has to be very careful with spending. 💳 🍕 🏃‍♂️

$100,000 (Tim): 🏘 Buys a nice condo, leases a luxury car, can afford high-end food, healthcare, and amenities. Able to save and invest. 🏢 🚗 🍱 🧘‍♂️

$25 million (Robert): 🏰 Owns a sprawling mansion with a staff, multiple luxury cars, private jet, and personal assistants. Spends freely on food, travel, and hobbies. Invests heavily to grow his wealth. 🤵‍♀️ ✈️ 🍷 🏏

The video highlights the growing wealth inequality in America and how the system is increasingly failing those in the lower income brackets.
