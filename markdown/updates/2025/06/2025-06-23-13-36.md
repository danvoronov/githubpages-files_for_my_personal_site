**LEGO isn’t just a toy** — it’s a psychological masterpiece

[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=b7I8OVXn7wY](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=b7I8OVXn7wY)

Despite being time-consuming, expensive, and sometimes frustrating, millions love it. Here’s why:

**🔨 1. The IKEA Effect**

We value things more when we build them ourselves! Research shows people pay extra for self-assembled items—even if imperfect. LEGO taps into this by making you feel pride in your creation. Your brain thinks: "I worked hard, so this must be special!"

**📊 2. The Progress Principle**

LEGO sets aren’t just dumped in one bag—they’re split into numbered steps. Each small win (like adding a wheel 🚗) gives a dopamine boost. Studies prove progress—not rewards—boosts happiness. LEGO turns building into a game of tiny victories!

**🌀 3. The Flow State**

Ever lost hours building? That’s flow—a mental state where challenge meets skill perfectly . LEGO provides:
+ Clear goals (the final build)
+ Instant feedback (pieces fitting)
+ Just-right difficulty (not too easy/hard)

Like Tetris or Minecraft, it’s addictively satisfying!
