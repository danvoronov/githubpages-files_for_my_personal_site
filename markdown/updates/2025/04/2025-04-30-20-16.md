Додаткова стаття
[https://invertedpassion.com/metathinking/](https://invertedpassion.com/metathinking/)

Significant life decisions are stressful partly because we lack training in rational decision-making. Our brains, evolved for survival, often settle for "good enough" solutions rather than optimal ones and are prone to cognitive biases like availability and confirmation bias, distorting our choices.

The solution is "metacognitive thinking": planning your thinking process before deciding.

Examples include using predefined criteria for choosing an office city to avoid external influence, and preparing specific topics for interviews to ensure focus.

This approach provides a structured plan, eliminating random factors and leading to well-founded decisions.

**The key is to define clear criteria before exploring options, ensuring conscious and effective choices.**
